Insights for Visualization 1
(Scatter plot: AVG repair cost + Repair Count by Category & Build Year
Bar chart: AVG repair cost by Region & Category)

Insights & Key Findings
1. Repair costs vary significantly by category across all build years.  
Damp & Mould and Roofing consistently appear among the most expensive categories, regardless of property age. This suggests these issues require more complex or resource‑intensive work.

2. Older properties tend to show higher repair counts.  
In the scatter plot, properties built before around 1970 have more data points with higher repair counts. This indicates that ageing buildings require more frequent maintenance.

3. Electrical and Plumbing repairs show lower average costs.  
Across most build years, these categories cluster at the lower end of the cost range. This may reflect simpler repair processes or more predictable work.

4. London shows higher average repair costs across multiple categories.  
In the regional bar chart, London consistently appears with higher average costs, especially for Damp & Mould and Roofing. This could be related to property age, labour costs, or building density.

5. Birmingham and Liverpool show more balanced cost distribution.  
These regions have moderate average repair costs across categories, without extreme spikes. This suggests more stable repair patterns.

6. “Missing” region values indicate incomplete data.  
The presence of a “Missing” region category in the chart highlights a data quality issue. Some repairs are not assigned to a region, which may affect accuracy of regional comparisons.

Insights for Visualization 2
(Repairs by Region, Avg Cost, Avg Resolution Days, Avg Satisfaction, Repair Count by Build Year)

Insights & Key Findings
1. London has the highest number of repairs.  
The region chart shows London clearly leading in repair volume. This may be due to a larger housing stock or older buildings.

2. Damp & Mould is the most expensive repair category.  
It stands out with the highest average repair cost compared to all other categories. This indicates complex work and potentially serious underlying issues.

3. Roofing and Heating have the longest resolution times.  
These categories show the highest average resolution days, suggesting they require more time, planning, or specialist contractors.

4. Electrical and Plumbing repairs are completed faster.  
These categories have the lowest resolution times, which aligns with higher satisfaction scores.

5. Satisfaction is strongly linked to resolution speed.  
Categories with shorter resolution times (Electrical, Plumbing) show higher satisfaction, while slower categories (Roofing, Heating) show lower satisfaction.

6. Older properties require more repairs.  
The scatter plot shows higher repair counts for properties built before 1970. Newer buildings (after 2000) show fewer repairs.

7. Missing region data indicates a data quality gap.  
The “Missing” region category suggests incomplete records. This should be addressed to improve accuracy of regional analysis.
